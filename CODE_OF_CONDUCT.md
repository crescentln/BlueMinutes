# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free community for
everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience, nationality,
language, disability, or professional role.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and constructive.
- Discuss ideas and evidence rather than attacking people.
- Respect privacy, confidentiality, and the project's sensitive-data rules.
- Accept responsibility for mistakes and correct them promptly.
- Prefer precise, accessible language and make space for contributors with
  different levels of technical or domain experience.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome attention, or deliberate
  intimidation.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Sharing real meeting material, diplomatic records, credentials, or other
  sensitive data in Issues, Pull Requests, discussions, or demonstrations.
- Conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional
  community.

## Scope and enforcement

This policy applies in project spaces and when representing the project in
public. Report conduct concerns privately to the primary maintainer through the
GitHub profile. Do not include sensitive project or meeting data. The maintainer
may edit or remove contributions, comments, or other material that violates
this policy and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately where
appropriate, and avoid unnecessary disclosure about reporters or affected
people.
